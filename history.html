History of Pizza

This web page focuses on how modern pizza took shape in Naples, how the famous “Margherita” story became part of pizza culture, and how traditions are still protected today.

Quick overview

Many cultures have made flatbreads with toppings, but the pizza we recognize today is most strongly connected with Naples, where it became an affordable, everyday food and later a global favorite.

Sources: Britannica, Smithsonian

Timeline highlights

  1. Naples becomes the home of modern pizza

    Over time, Naples developed a strong pizza tradition and a culture of pizza makers, known as “pizzaioli”, helped shape what many people think of as classic pizza today.

    Sources: Britannica, Smithsonian

  2. 1889 — The “Margherita” story becomes famous

    A widely repeated story connects the toppings tomato (red), mozzarella (white), and basil (green) to Queen Margherita of Savoy and the colors of the Italian flag. This connection is often described as a popular attribution rather than a fully proven origin for pizza itself.

    Sources: Britannica, La Cucina Italiana

  3. 1905 — Pizza spreads internationally (example: United States)

    As Italian immigrants brought their food traditions abroad, pizza gained new audiences. One well-known milestone is the opening of a licensed pizzeria in New York in the early 1900s.

    Source: Britannica

  4. After World War II — popularity grows even more

    Pizza's popularity expanded quickly after World War II, which also included in the United States, and regional styles developed overtime.

    Source: Britannica

  5. 1984 — AVPN forms to protect true Neapolitan pizza

    In Naples, a group of pizza makers formed the Associazione Verace Pizza Napoletana (AVPN) and registered rules meant to protect and preserve “true Neapolitan pizza.”

    Source: AVPN

Key figures

Queen Margherita of Savoy

Italian queen associated with the Margherita pizza story

The classic tomato-mozzarella-basil combination is popularly linked to Queen Margherita and the Italian flag colors. On many timelines, this story represents pizza's shift from street food into a symbol of national pride, even though pizza existed before the Margherita legend.

Source: Britannica

Raffaele Esposito

Neapolitan pizzaiolo often credited in the 1889 Margherita story

The creation of Pizza Margherita is often attributed to Raffaele Esposito in 1889, where he is said to have prepared pizzas in honor of Queen Margherita of Savoy. This is a famous and meaningful origin story, but it's best described as an “often attributed” tradition rather than the beginning of pizza history.

Source: La Cucina Italiana
